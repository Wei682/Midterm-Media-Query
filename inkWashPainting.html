Ink Wash Painting

History

Northern School ink wash painting

Southern School ink wash painting

Ink wash painting was created in the Tang dynasty (618-907). As it developed, two distinct styles were identified in the Ming dynastry (1368-1644): a clearer, grander Northern School and a freer, more expressive Southern School.

Technique

Ink wash painting achieves tonal variations and shading by controlling the ink and water ratio and the pressure within each single brushstroke. A traditional black stroke can produce colors ranging from deep black to silvery gray.

Philosophy

Ink wash painting emphasizes on capturing the spirit over reproducing the appearance. For example, to paint a horse, it is more important for the artist to understand its temperament better than its muscles and bones. For another instance, when painting a flower, it's not necessary to perfectly replicate its petals and colors; instead, it's crucial to convey its liveliness and fragrance.
